Reviews are how moving customers decide who to trust.

Before anyone calls you, they have already read what other people said. A wall of five-star reviews wins jobs before you have spoken a word.

So here is the strange part: most moving companies do great work and barely ask for them.

## Why great moves don't always get reviews

A happy customer rarely leaves a review on their own.

Not because they weren't happy. Because the move is over, they are unpacking, life moves on, and writing a review is the last thing on their mind. The good feeling fades within a day or two.

The window to catch that feeling is short. Miss it, and a job that earned a five-star review gets no review at all.

## Asking feels awkward, so it gets skipped

The other reason is that asking face to face feels uncomfortable.

The crew just finished a hard day's work. Standing there asking the customer to go online and praise them feels like a lot. So they don't. Or the owner means to follow up later and forgets in the rush of the next job.

Either way, the ask doesn't happen, and the review doesn't come.

## The timing that works

The best moment to ask is right after the move, while the relief and gratitude are fresh.

The customer just watched their whole life get moved safely. That is the high point. A simple, friendly message then, with a direct link that makes leaving a review take ten seconds, catches them at exactly the right time.

Wait a week and the moment is gone. Ask at the peak and people say yes.

## Make the ask happen on its own

The reason this works for top companies is that they don't rely on remembering.

The request goes out automatically once a job is marked done. In your tone, with the link right there, at the moment that works. No awkward face-to-face ask. No owner trying to remember which customers to chase.

The move ends, the message goes, the reviews come in. Quietly building the wall of trust that wins your next jobs.

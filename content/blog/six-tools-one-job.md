We have spent the last few months talking with moving company owners across Denmark, Sweden, and Norway. The pattern is the same in almost every conversation: nobody likes their software, and most are running on a stack of five to seven different tools that do not talk to each other.

This post is what that costs you, why it happens, and how we are thinking about it.

## The typical setup

Pull apart any moving company in the Nordics and you usually find some version of this:

* A spreadsheet for quoting
* WhatsApp for crew coordination
* A shared Google Calendar for scheduling
* e-conomic or Dinero for invoicing and bookkeeping
* A separate CRM, or a folder of email threads pretending to be one
* Personal phones for customer calls
* A whiteboard or paper printout for the day's jobs

Each tool was added because the previous one fell short. Nobody set out to design this. It just happened, one fix at a time.

## What it costs

The cost is not the subscriptions. The cost is the friction between the tools. Every job is a series of context switches:

1. Customer fills in a contact form on your website.
2. You copy the details into your spreadsheet to build a quote.
3. You email the quote.
4. Customer accepts. You add the booking to the calendar.
5. You message the crew on WhatsApp with the address and time.
6. Day of the job, you call the lead to confirm everyone has the right info.
7. After the job, you create the invoice in your accounting tool.
8. You chase payment from your inbox.

Eight steps, four tools, zero automation. And that is the happy path. When something changes (a new address, a different crew, a customer rescheduling), the change has to be made in three or four places. One missed update and two crews show up to the wrong house.

> "We were spending more time managing the tools than managing the jobs." -- Owner of a 12-person Copenhagen moving company

That is what most owners we talk to say, in slightly different words.

## Why generic software does not solve this

The obvious answer is "use a CRM" or "use ServiceM8" or "use any of the field service tools." We hear this a lot. It does not work.

Generic field service software was built for plumbers, electricians, and HVAC technicians. The data model is wrong for movers:

* Plumbers do not need a multi-stop route with packing time at origin, transit time, and unpacking at destination.
* HVAC techs do not need to price by volume, distance, floor, and crew size at the same time.
* Electricians do not need a customer portal that holds an inventory list with photos.

You can bend a generic tool to fit, but the workflow always feels off, and the people doing the work feel it first.

## What we are building

Movena is one platform for the entire moving job:

* Quotes priced from your own rates, sent in minutes
* Bookings that flow into a single calendar
* Crews that see their jobs in a mobile app, no daily phone calls needed
* A customer portal that shows the inventory, schedule, and cost
* Invoicing that connects to e-conomic and Dinero so your books stay clean

The thesis is simple. If everything happens in one place, the friction disappears. The owner sees what is happening without asking. The crew shows up where they should be. The customer feels like they are dealing with a real company, not a thread of WhatsApp messages.
